# Deckbuilding mindset

- Solve the **next** loss condition first—often the upcoming elite or boss, not abstract endgame fantasy. Ask what kills you in the **next several floors**, not whether a card “fits the build.”
- **Deck density** beats raw card count: fewer dead draws; removals are often tempo-positive. Don’t force a **named archetype** on floor 1—rewards may never ship the synergy pieces; add cards that answer the **map and encounters** you see.
- **Enabler / engine / payload**: enablers give **energy or draw**; **engines** are powers or compounding value over the fight; **payloads** are big damage or block that uses that state. Take payloads only when enablers exist. Late acts: deck may need to **grow slightly** to dilute status junk (Wound, Dazed).
- **Scaling needs delivery**: draw and energy multiply good cards; scaling with no draw stalls.
- Card roles: front-loaded damage, AoE, draw/cycle, combo payoffs vs engines—take payoffs only when outlets exist.
- **Neow**: *Neow's Lament* (next 3 combats enemies at 1 HP) applies to normal, elite, and boss—preview the map before picking blessings that assume a snipe. Random rare is high variance.
- **Swapping starter for boss relic** reframes the run—weight character reliance on starter relic before gambling.
- **Boss-specific removal**: e.g. **Slime Boss** split timing can want granular **Strike** damage; **Guardian** thorns phases can value **Defends**—revisit merchant/event removals when those fights are next.
